## 2. Structural Acceleration Thesis

AI-native systems increase implementation velocity beyond human structural oversight capacity.

Generation throughput now exceeds structural validation throughput.

When structural change outpaces structural governance, systems accumulate:

* Implicit transitions
* Undeclared evolution
* Migration ambiguity
* Contract instability

This condition produces structural entropy.

Rigor exists as a counterbalance to structural acceleration.

---

## 3. Structural Entropy (Definition)

Structural Entropy is defined as:

> The progressive increase of implicit, unclassified, or non-deterministic structural behavior across iterative system evolution.

Structural entropy manifests when:

* Transitions are inferred instead of declared.
* Version changes are untyped.
* Migrations alter behavior without formal classification.
* Execution diverges from documented intent.

Structural entropy is cumulative.

Without constraint, it becomes irreversible.

Rigor positions itself as an entropy-reduction protocol.

---

## 4. Category Definition

Rigor defines a new category:

> AI Constraint Protocol

An AI Constraint Protocol is:

A pre-execution structural boundary system that constrains, validates, and classifies AI-generated structural evolution before runtime.

This category is distinct from:

* Orchestration platforms
* Runtime engines
* Infrastructure definition tools
* Domain-specific languages
* Static analysis systems

Rigor operates upstream of execution.
