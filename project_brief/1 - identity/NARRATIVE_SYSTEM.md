# NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md — v0.1

**Rigor — Narrative System Specification**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how Rigor communicates.

It establishes:

* Linguistic constraints.
* Structural tone.
* Narrative hierarchy.
* Approved conceptual vocabulary.
* Prohibited framing patterns.

The narrative system must preserve strategic positioning defined in prior documents.

Communication that violates this document weakens identity coherence.

---

## 2. Narrative Principles

Rigor communicates as a protocol specification, not as a product brand.

The narrative must be:

* Declarative
* Precise
* Structural
* Non-emotional
* Non-promotional

Rigor does not persuade.
Rigor defines.

---

## 3. Tone Characteristics

### 3.1 Formal, Not Corporate

Avoid corporate language.

Avoid growth language.

Avoid motivational framing.

Correct:

> Rigor defines structural boundaries.

Incorrect:

> Rigor empowers teams to innovate safely.

---

### 3.2 Deterministic, Not Conversational

Avoid casual tone.

Avoid rhetorical questions.

Avoid storytelling arcs.

Correct:

> Generation without constraints produces structural entropy.

Incorrect:

> Have you ever struggled with evolving systems?

---

### 3.3 Restrained Authority

Rigor speaks with structural certainty.

It does not exaggerate.

It does not claim superiority.

Correct:

> Validation precedes execution.

Incorrect:

> The most advanced solution for AI systems.

---

## 4. Narrative Structure Pattern

Public-facing sections must follow this sequence:

1. Structural condition
2. Constraint principle
3. Resulting guarantee

Example:

> AI increases generation velocity.
> Unconstrained generation produces structural entropy.
> Rigor defines formal boundaries.

This pattern must be preserved across landing, documentation, and publications.

---

## 5. Core Vocabulary

Approved terminology:

* Constraint
* Deterministic
* Explicit
* Structural
* Boundary
* Protocol
* Evolution
* Validation
* Version-classified
* Boundedness
* Entropy (structural context only)

These words define conceptual territory.

---

## 6. Prohibited Vocabulary

The following must not appear in official communication:

* Fast
* Easy
* Seamless
* Disruptive
* Revolutionary
* Cutting-edge
* Magic
* Smart
* Empower
* Boost productivity
* Supercharge

These words shift positioning toward marketing categories Rigor does not belong to.

---

## 7. Sentence Construction Rules

* Prefer short declarative sentences.
* Avoid metaphor unless structural and technical.
* Avoid emotional adjectives.
* Avoid exclamation marks.
* Avoid hype-based claims.

Rigor favors structural clarity over stylistic expression.

---

## 8. Hero Section Pattern

Landing hero must follow this structure:

Primary Line:

RIGOR
AI Constraint Protocol.

Secondary Line:

Formal boundaries for AI-generated systems.

Optional Third Line:

Generation requires boundaries.

No feature list in hero.
No benefit framing.

---

## 9. Positioning Language Constraints

When referencing adjacent systems such as:

* Temporal
* AWS Step Functions

Language must remain structural.

Correct:

> These systems orchestrate execution.
> Rigor constrains structural possibility before execution.

Incorrect:

> Unlike Temporal, Rigor is better for AI systems.

Rigor does not compete rhetorically.
It differentiates structurally.

---

## 10. AI Framing Rules

Rigor must never be framed as:

* Anti-AI
* AI replacement
* AI enhancement layer
* AI performance optimizer

Correct framing:

> Rigor constrains AI-generated structural behavior.

AI is not adversarial.

AI is bounded.

---

## 11. Narrative Invariants

The following statements must remain stable across communications:

* Validation precedes execution.
* Evolution must be classified.
* Determinism is a requirement.
* Generation requires boundaries.

These phrases form identity anchors.

---

## 12. Emotional Temperature

Rigor’s emotional temperature is neutral.

It does not celebrate.

It does not dramatize.

It does not warn with urgency.

It defines structural necessity.

---

## 13. Long-Term Narrative Stability

As Rigor evolves:

* Terminology may expand.
* Examples may diversify.
* Implementation references may increase.

But tone must remain:

Protocol-level, not product-level.
