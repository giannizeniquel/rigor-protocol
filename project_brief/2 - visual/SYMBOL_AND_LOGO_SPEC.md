# SYMBOL_AND_LOGO_SPEC.md — v0.1

**Rigor — Symbol and Logo Specification**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines:

* The structural logic of the Rigor wordmark.
* The geometric rules governing its construction.
* The potential symbol system.
* Usage constraints and invariants.

The logo must express boundedness, determinism, and structural authority.

It must not express personality.

---

## 2. Core Logo Philosophy

The Rigor logo is not expressive.
It is declarative.

It must communicate:

* Structural containment.
* Formal constraint.
* Technical precision.
* Institutional neutrality.

The logo must feel engineered, not designed.

---

## 3. Wordmark Specification

### 3.1 Typography Character

The wordmark must follow these principles:

* Serif or controlled semi-serif structure.
* No script characteristics.
* No rounded softness.
* No exaggerated contrast.
* No decorative terminals.

Letterforms must appear constructed.

---

### 3.2 Case Rules

Primary form:

RIGOR

Alternate (secondary contexts only):

Rigor

Lowercase-only usage is prohibited.

---

### 3.3 Kerning and Spacing

Tracking must be slightly expanded.

Spacing intent:

* Letters must not touch.
* No tight kerning.
* Breathing space must communicate separation and structure.

Spacing must feel intentional and mechanical.

---

## 4. Structural Containment Variant

A containment version may be defined:

```
[RIGOR]
```

or

RIGOR enclosed in a thin rectangular boundary.

Rules:

* Stroke weight minimal.
* No rounded corners.
* Rectangle must align exactly to baseline and cap height.
* Padding equal on all sides.

This version expresses explicit constraint.

---

## 5. Symbol Exploration System

A symbol is optional. If used, it must follow strict geometry.

### 5.1 Symbol Intent

The symbol must represent:

* Constraint.
* Structural boundary.
* Deterministic transformation.
* Rule enforcement.

It must not represent:

* AI brains.
* Neural networks.
* Abstract organic forms.
* Infinite loops.

---

### 5.2 Acceptable Symbol Concepts

Concept 1 — Framed Square

A perfect square with internal subdivided grid lines.

Represents bounded execution space.

Concept 2 — Nested Rectangles

Multiple aligned rectangular outlines.

Represents layered constraint.

Concept 3 — Constrained Arrow

An arrow enclosed in a boundary box.

Represents directed transformation under limits.

Concept 4 — Parallel Bars

Three vertical bars inside a frame.

Represents structural rules.

---

### 5.3 Geometry Rules

If a symbol is created:

* Must be based on a square grid.
* No curves.
* No diagonal decorative angles unless structurally justified.
* Uniform stroke weight.
* No filled shapes by default.

The symbol must feel schematic.

---

## 6. Color Application Rules

Primary logo:

* Controlled White on dark background.

Secondary:

* Deep Blue accent for symbol or underline only.

Forbidden:

* Gradient fills.
* Multi-color logo.
* Glow effects.
* 3D shading.

---

## 7. Clear Space Rules

Minimum clear space:

Height of the letter “R” on all sides.

No element may intrude into this zone.

This preserves structural isolation.

---

## 8. Minimum Size

Minimum digital display:

* 24px height for wordmark.
* 32px if symbol included.

Below this size, containment variant is preferred for legibility.

---

## 9. Improper Usage

The following are prohibited:

* Stretching or scaling disproportionately.
* Adding drop shadows.
* Adding rounded corners to containment.
* Changing letter case.
* Recoloring outside defined palette.
* Applying decorative typography.

Deviation weakens authority.

---

## 10. Logotype Lockups

Allowed lockups:

1. Wordmark alone.
2. Symbol above wordmark.
3. Symbol left of wordmark (aligned vertically center).
4. Containment variant alone.

Stacked lockups must maintain strict alignment.

No diagonal compositions.

---

## 11. Tone Alignment

The logo must align with:

* AI Constraint Protocol philosophy.
* Formal specification language.
* Structural communication.

It must not resemble:

* SaaS growth brands.
* AI startups with organic branding.
* Futuristic neon aesthetics.
* Tech minimalism clichés.

---

## 12. Evolution Constraints

The following are invariant:

* Capitalized primary form.
* Structural geometry.
* Neutral authority tone.
* Dark-dominant presentation.

Symbol may evolve.
Wordmark structure must remain stable.
